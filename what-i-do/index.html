Some of my knowledge and skills

Due to the vast number of expert areas a web developer can belong to, it's probably best if I briefly outline my current skills and knowledge base to help you make a decision whether I can help out on your project.

Front-end development

I'm primarily a front end developer, which means for the most part I will be focusing on writing semantic HTML markup, scalable and reusable CSS, and effective JavaScript. These are the coding languages I put in practice to create the interfaces that users will see and interact with. I really love digging into code, which is why all of my work is handcrafted by yours truly; using templates or frameworks such as Bootstrap just isn't my thing. I try to build projects with enriched with semantic value, suitably for the current and future scope of any project.

Responsive web design

Users should be given a positive experience, no matter how they decide to view your website. This is where the beauty of responsive design comes in. Through the use of fluid grid systems, your website can be designed to respond and adapt to the needs of most users, making it look good and function across a range of devices; from wide screen monitors to mobile phones. This is why I love the idea of responsive design, and like to focus on techniques to help build better, well-executed websites that can be used effectively on a range of different devices and be accessible to users of different capabilities and needs.

Back-end development

This process focuses on how the website works in the backend, and how everything pieces together on the server in terms of data and logic. I have a well-rounded knowledge of using PHP and have used this language to both extend and create customized WordPress themes, and have also used PHP to build custom Content Management Systems (CMS) from scratch using the framework CodeIgniter.

So whether you're after a simple blog powered by WordPress or a custom content management solution, I can be of assistance.

User Interface design

Ugly websites? Ain't nobody got time for that! That's why it's important to design a good looking, usable interface that will be appropriate for your users. This process often starts with rough sketches and wireframes, leading into designing visual prototypes in Photoshop or designing straight in the browser.
