Solicitor website (Concept work)

A small dynamic law firm recently got in touch with me about helping them to re-think their current website. Excited by the possibilities and ideas I had going through my mind, I decided to make a start without first winning the bid for the project.

Out of the 11 different designers, developers & agencies they got in touch with, they narrowed their choice down to me and one other agency. Unfortunately I didn't get the oppertunity to work on this project for unenquired reasons, but I decided to keep going with what I started.

Sketching & Wireframing

As this project didn't start as being commissioned work, I dived straight into designing the visual prototypes without sketching out any wireframes. I had a strong idea as to where I wanted to go with the website visually, so didn't feel the need to sketch out my thoughts.

Website Development (Build)

Now the groundwork has been completed and we have an agreed spec to work from it’s time for me to get creative. I start by sketching. This helps me to get rough ideas down for the project - whether this is to do with layout, typography or something else.
