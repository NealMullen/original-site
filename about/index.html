A little more about myself

As you may have already read, I'm Neal Mullen, a web designer and developer based just outside Edinburgh, Scotland. I've had an interest in the web for many years now, but have spent the past 5 years or so dedicated to finding out how the web works, and learning about the skills and techniques required to build websites and applications.

For the most part I've been working as a small-time freelancer, who's been lucky enough to work on a range of different projects with some really interesting people. I've been personally invited to work with the lovely folks at Kalexiko Studio over the Summer in 2012, and have recently started contracting in Edinburgh for Realise Ltd where I've been lucky enough to work on projects for some exciting clients, such as Martin & Co and the BBC.

Designing is cool, but I love to code

There is nothing like sinking your teeth into a bit of code - it's undoubtedly is my favourite part of any web project. I love the problem solving aspect that is often involved in any web project, and coming up with a effective and efficient solution to any problem gives me great satisfaction. For me, the projects with the most challenging issues often turn out to be the projects that I enjoy working on the most (even if it makes me cry on occasions).

I mainly work on front-end builds using HTML5, CSS3 and JavaScript, but I'm more than confident using PHP when it comes to building websites in the likes of WordPress, CodeIgniter, or even building simple Content Management Systems from scratch.

Theres too much I want to learn!

There is always something new happening is the web industry - whether it's new CSS pre-processing techniques, or new ways to build websites and applications. Due to this, my "Things to learn" list is constantly growing. Unfortunately it's hard to find the time to learn everything I want. Lately however, I have been dipping my toes into likes of Grunt JS, Ruby on Rails and Angular JS. Look over my current knowledge and skills.

I love working on new projects

No matter if you're a start-up or a small local business, the opportunity to work on new projects and collaborate with new people gets my creative juices flowing. I enjoy the prospect of improving my skills with every project, and delivering a result that both I and my clients are happy with. So if you have a project in mind, or want to get me on board with something you're working on yourself, please feel free to get in touch and we can have a chat.
